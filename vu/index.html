Mount Kilimanjaro is the highest mountain in Africa and the tallest freestanding mountain on earth. Crowned with an everlasting snow-cap, this majestic mountain can be found inside the Kilimanjaro National Park of Tanzania. The summit on Mount Kilimanjaro is called Uhuru Peak and stands at 5,895m or 19,341 feet.

Mont Blanc is the highest summit of Western Europe, on the border between France and Italy. The rock 1 peak of Mont Blanc is 4792 m high, but this is overlain by a thick cap of snow and ice that grows or shrinks slightly according to climate. Mont Blanc is not the highest mountain in Europe as several peaks in the Russian / Georgian Caucasus are higher, culminating in Elbrus at 5642 m.
Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth. Mount Everest is in the Himalayas, the highest mountain range in the world. It is about 8,848.00 metres (29,028.87 ft) high. Its peak is on the border of Nepal and China. It is above the Death Zone where the air is too thin for a human being to live, so usually compressed gas tanks with different gas mixes for different altitudes are used when climbing. The Death Zone refers to the parts of Mount Everest that are above 7,600 metres (24,900 ft) above sea level. Everest is called Chomolungma by the Tibetan people.
